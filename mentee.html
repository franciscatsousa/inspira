Who can be a mentee?

Women undergraduate, master's, or doctoral students enrolled in the Department of Informatics Engineering, as well as early-career female professors or researchers.Â Mentees should be interested in enhancing key skills relevant to academic and career development and should be committed and motivated to participate in mentoring sessions and other activities.

What does a mentee need to do?

Attend both individual and group mentoring sessions and ensure prompt initiation of contact with mentor. If unable to attend a session or regular contact, the mentee must communicate this to the mentor beforehand. Mentees should be receptive to guidance, actively set and pursue the established goals, and uphold confidentiality throughout the program.

What can a mentee expect from the program?

Receiving personalized guidance from experienced colleagues, helping them develop skills essential for career development, while facilitating reflection on career options, planning, and progression. skills for their careers. Mentees can also receive advice on work-life balance, navigating professional challenges, and potential gender issues within the field of informatics. Moreover, mentees will contact with female role models in the field of Informatics.
