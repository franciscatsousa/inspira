alright, and this is how

Attract, support, and retain female students, researchers, and professors in Informatics.

Through its mentoring activities, INSPIRA provides tailored support to mentees. Through a supportive environment, we support women address challenges, leverage opportunities, and thrive academically and professionally.

Foster an academic environment which prioritizes and includes everyone.

INSPIRA is committed to promoting inclusivity, embracing diversity, and cultivating a supportive and nurturing environment that, aware of gender issues, strives to ensure the comfort, empowerment, and success of our academic community.

Create a space for sharing experiences, acquiring skills, and connecting with mentors and peers.

By creating opportunities for guidance, support, and establishing meaningful connections with mentors and peers, our aim is to empower individuals to realize their full potential and pursue their goals confidently and resiliently.

Mentoring is our focus

What is “mentoring”?

Mentoring is generally understood as a sustained relationship between a mentor and a mentee.

In this relationship, a more experienced individual (mentor) supports the academic and/or professional development of a less experienced one (mentee) through listening, reflection, counseling, and training skills, identifying, and working on strengths and weaknesses.

Who’s involved and how is the matching done?

The INSPIRA team regularly receives applications from both mentees and mentors.

Applicants are asked about their motivations for participating in the program, the goals they hope to achieve, and the characteristics they seek in a mentor or mentee. Matches are made based on the analysis of these responses. The INSPIRA team then notifies the mentors and mentees during an initial ice-breaking session.

How long does a mentoring cycle last & what format does it take?

Each mentoring cycle lasts throughout the academic year and there are two main mentoring formats:

Individual mentoring: mentees engage in personalized mentoring sessions after arranging a convenient schedule with their mentors.

Group mentoring: these are organised as events open to everyone that may take the form of workshops, seminars, or discussion groups. These sessions cover various topics such as career strategic planning, negotiation, presentation skills, personal branding, and networking.

What is
inspira-balance?

It is a project dedicated to promoting gender balance in informatics. It has received funding from the First Foundation Project, which stems from a collaboration between Feedzai and the University of Coimbra.
Goals:

Is to raise awareness about the importance of gender balance in informatics; encourage more women to pursue, enter, and thrive in careers in tech; make a lasting impact on the perception and reality of gender diversity in the tech industry.

The support from the First Foundation Project will enable us to undertake a series of engaging activities designed to foster inclusion within the tech sector, through which INSPIRA-BALANCE will encourage individuals to step out of their comfort zones.
Activities:
Round Tables:featuring IT professionals sharing their experiences and insights.
Workshops: Hands-on sessions to raise awareness about diversity and inclusion.
Contests:Encouraging and highlighting innovative projects by women in IT through competitions.
Interviews:Collecting testimonials to better understand the job market.
We are grateful to the First Foundation Project for their generous support, which makes these initiatives possible. Join us in creating a more inclusive and diverse tech industry!
