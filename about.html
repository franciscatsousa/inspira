Want do we want to do?

Attract, support and retain female students, researchers, and professors in Informatics at the University of Coimbra.

Our program offers tailored support to address their unique challenges and opportunities. Through a supportive environment and targeted resources, we empower women in the Informatics community, fostering their academic and professional growth.

Create an academic environment prioritizing sensitivity and awareness of gender issues, fostering a feeling of confort, empowerment, and thriving for all students. By promoting inclusivity and embracing diverse perspectives, we strive for richer discourse, innovative thinking, and positive societal change.

Create a space dedicated to the exchange of experiences, acquisition of essential skills, and access to mentors who offer invaluable guidance and support. By facilitating meaningful connections and fostering a supportive learning environment, we aim to empower individuals to unlock their full potential and achieve their goals with confidence and resilienceok.

Mentoring is our focus
1. what is that “mentoring”?
Mentoring is understood as a sustained relationship, not a one-off or casual one, between a mentor and a mentee.

In this relationship, a more experienced individual (mentor) supports the academic and/or professional development of a less experienced one (mentee) through listening, reflection, counseling, and training skills, identifying and working on strengths and weaknesses.

2. who’s involved and how is the match done?
The Inspira team receives applications from both mentees and mentors. Subsequently, we analyze the form asnwers, which inquire about the reasons for wanting to participate in the program, the goals they wish to achieve, and the type of person they would like to work with. Based on these responses, we, the Inspira team, make the matches and then notify the participants during an initial ice-breaking session.

3. How long is a mentoring cycle and what is required?
Each edition should last the academic year, offering two main mentoring formats:

Individual mentoring: Mentees engage in personalized mentoring sessions after arranging a convenient schedule with their mentors.

Group mentoring: sessions, organized as events within our program, may take the form of workshops or discussion groups. They cover various topics such as career strategic planning, negotiation, presentation skills, personal branding, and networking.
